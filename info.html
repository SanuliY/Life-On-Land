A World in Balance with Nature

Healthy forests and ecosystems provide shelter, food, and resources for millions of species, including humans.Protecting them ensures that future generations can thrive on a sustainable planet

Why Protecting Forests Matters

Forests are home to more than 80% of terrestrial animals, plants, and fungi. They regulate our climate, absorb carbon dioxide, and prevent soil erosion. Losing them meanslosing our best defense against climate change.

Biodiversity Facts

  • Over 1.6 billion people depend on forests for their livelihoods.

  • Around 8,400 species of wild fauna and flora are critically endangered.

  • Protecting biodiversity helps maintain food security and natural balance.
